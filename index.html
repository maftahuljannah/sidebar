beef steak

A beefsteak, often called just steak, is a flat cut of beef with parallel faces, usually cut
perpendicular to the muscle fibers. In common restaurant service a single serving
has a raw mass ranging from 120 to 600 grams. Beef steaks are
usually grilled, pan-fried, or broiled.
